# Sector-Specific Metrics for Indian Stocks

When analyzing a company, always look at sector-appropriate metrics in addition to the general framework. Here are the key ones for India's major investable sectors.

---

## Banking & NBFCs

Banks and NBFCs are valued differently from normal companies — don't use standard P/E or Debt/Equity frameworks.

| Metric | What It Measures | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| **NIM** (Net Interest Margin) | Profitability of core lending (spread between lending and deposit rates) | 3–4%+ is healthy; HDFC Bank ~4%+ |
| **GNPA %** | Gross Non-Performing Assets / Total Loans | < 2% is good; > 5% is a serious concern |
| **NNPA %** | Net NPA (after provisions made) | < 1% is ideal |
| **PCR** (Provision Coverage Ratio) | How much of NPAs are covered by provisions | 70%+ is considered healthy |
| **CASA Ratio** | Low-cost (Current + Savings) deposits / Total deposits | 40–50%+ is excellent (lower cost of funds) |
| **CAR** (Capital Adequacy Ratio) | RBI requires min 11.5%; buffer above that = resilience | 15%+ is comfortable |
| **RoA** (Return on Assets) | Profitability vs total assets (key bank metric, not ROE) | > 1% good; > 2% excellent (rare) |
| **Credit Cost** | Provisions made as % of loan book | Lower is better; spikes signal NPA trouble |
| **Loan Book Growth** | Growth in advances / AUM | Sustainable 15–20%+ YoY is positive |
| **Cost-to-Income Ratio** | Operating efficiency | < 50% is good |
| **P/B Ratio** | Banks are valued on book value, not earnings | Good banks trade at 2-4x P/B; great ones at 5x+ |

**For NBFCs, additionally track:**
- AUM (Assets Under Management) growth
- Cost of Funds vs Yield on Assets (spread)
- Secured vs Unsecured loan mix (unsecured = higher risk and margin)

## IT Services (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL, mid-cap IT)

Indian IT is export-driven — understand currency effects and deal cycles.

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Revenue Growth (CC — Constant Currency)** | Strips out rupee depreciation effect; actual business growth |
| **EBIT Margin** | Typically 20–25% for tier-1 IT; pressured by wage hikes and attrition |
| **TCV of Deal Wins** (Total Contract Value) | Forward revenue visibility; large deal TCV ($50M+) is especially important |
| **Employee Utilization Rate** | How efficiently headcount is used (higher = better efficiency) |
| **Attrition Rate** | Talent retention; stabilized at ~12–13% after FY22-23 spike |
| **Revenue per Employee** | Productivity and pricing power |
| **Headcount Growth** | Leading indicator of revenue trajectory (hiring before revenue) |
| **Revenue Mix: Digital vs Legacy** | Higher digital = higher growth + better margin |
| **Client Concentration** | % from top 5/10 clients (high concentration = risk) |
| **Deal Pipeline / Large Deal Wins** | Signals 2-3 year revenue visibility |

**Currency context**: Rupee depreciation (INR/USD) is a tailwind for IT exporters — boosts reported INR revenues. Track dollar-revenue growth separately.

## FMCG (HUL, ITC, Nestle, Dabur, Trent, DMart)

India's consumption story. Key is whether real demand is growing or just inflation-driven.

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Volume Growth** | Real demand growth (strip out price increases) — most honest measure |
| **Value Growth** | Volume growth + price hike; currently ~13-14% for leaders |
| **Rural vs Urban Split** | Rural India recovering faster; 7-8% volume growth vs 5-6% urban |
| **Distribution Reach** | Number of outlets directly covered; rural direct reach is a moat |
| **Gross Margin %** | Ability to absorb raw material cost increases without sacrificing profit |
| **A&P Spend** (Advertising & Promotion) | Brand investment as % of sales; consistent spenders build moats |
| **Market Share by Category** | Gaining or losing share? More important than absolute growth |
| **Premiumization Trend** | Mix shift toward premium SKUs signals pricing power |
| **Working Capital / Cash Conversion** | Best FMCG companies have negative working capital (get paid before they pay suppliers) |

## Pharmaceuticals (Sun Pharma, Dr. Reddy's, Cipla, mid-cap pharma)

Pharma has complex regulatory and product-mix dynamics.

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Domestic Formulations Growth** | Core India market; more stable and high-margin |
| **US Generics Revenue** | Usually highest-margin segment; but volatile due to price erosion |
| **ANDA Filings (pending/approved)** | Future US pipeline; more approvals = more products to launch |
| **US FDA Plant Status** | Critical — any 483 observations, Warning Letters, or Import Alerts = revenue at risk |
| **R&D Spend as % of Revenue** | Investment in future; look for 6-8%+ for innovation-focused companies |
| **Complex Generics / Specialty Pipeline** | Injectables, oncology, ophthalmology = less competition, better pricing |
| **EBITDA Margin** | 20–25% for integrated pharma; varies significantly by US/India mix |
| **Geographic Revenue Mix** | US, Europe, India, emerging markets — diversification reduces risk |
| **Patent Cliff Exposure** | How much revenue depends on a single product facing generic competition soon? |

**Key risk for Indian pharma**: US FDA warning letters can shut entire plants, wiping out 20-30% of revenue overnight. Always check FDA status before investing.

## Specialty Chemicals (PI Industries, Navin Fluorine, Aarti Industries, Deepak Nitrite)

One of India's most exciting sectors due to China+1 theme.

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Capacity Utilization %** | How full the plant is; high utilization = operating leverage |
| **Spread/Margin** | Price of finished chemical vs raw material input price |
| **China+1 Order Wins** | New multi-year contracts from global companies relocating supply chains from China |
| **Export Mix %** | Higher export = better pricing power and differentiation |
| **New Product Launches** | Pipeline diversity; over-reliance on 1-2 products is a risk |
| **Capex Cycle** | Are they expanding capacity? Signals future revenue growth (but short-term cash burn) |
| **Order Book / CSM (Contract) vs Commodity Mix** | CSM (contract manufacturing) is more stable and higher margin |

---

## Capital Goods & Infrastructure (L&T, ABB, Siemens, Cummins, KEC)

Government-capex linked; order book visibility is key.

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Order Inflows** | New orders received in the period — best leading indicator |
| **Order Book / Revenue ratio** | 2-3x is healthy; gives 2-3 years of revenue visibility |
| **Order Book Growth** | Expanding order book = future revenue growth locked in |
| **EBITDA Margin on Execution** | Margins realized vs bid margins (cost overruns are common) |
| **Working Capital Days** | Capital goods have long cycles; high WC = cash flow stress |
| **Debtor Days** | Government projects often delay payments; high debtor days is normal but monitor trends |
| **International Revenue Mix** | Diversification from India's capex cycle |

---

## Real Estate (Prestige, Sobha, DLF, Godrej Properties)

Asset-intensive, highly leverage-driven; completely different valuation framework.

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Pre-sales / Bookings** | Forward revenue and cash flow indicator |
| **Collections** | Actual cash converted from bookings (more important than pre-sales) |
| **Net Debt / EBITDA** | Leverage; should be < 2-3x for safety |
| **Unsold Inventory (quarters of sales)** | Demand health; < 8 quarters is acceptable |
| **Land Bank** | Future development potential |
| **RERA Compliance** | State RERA filings are mandatory; check project status |

## Quick Sector P/E Reference (approximate, as of 2025-26)

| Sector | Typical P/E Range |
|--------|------------------|
| IT (large cap) | 22–30x |
| FMCG | 40–60x |
| Banking (large) | 15–25x (or 2-4x P/B) |
| Pharma | 20–35x |
| Specialty Chemicals | 25–45x |
| Capital Goods | 30–50x |
| Auto | 20–30x |
| Consumer Discretionary | 30–60x |

A stock trading below sector P/E isn't necessarily cheap — understand WHY before assuming it's a bargain.
